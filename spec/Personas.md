## Primary Persona: The Newcomer

**Who:** Someone in Kyiv recommended by an existing participant and approved by the organizer. No prior context beyond what the recommender described. Could be any age, profession, or creative background. The only constant: they said yes to something unfamiliar.

**Goal:** Participate in something genuine and meet people outside their existing social circle, without the awkwardness of cold networking or the performance pressure of social media.

**Constraints:** Limited time (~2 weeks to create something). Uncertain what's expected. May have no "creative" self-image. Shipping costs money (~100 UAH / ~$2–3). Must physically visit Nova Poshta. Must share their phone number with an unknown sender (part of the trust contract).

**Fears:** "What if what I make is stupid?" "What if everyone else is more creative?" "What if this is weird?" "A stranger will have my phone number?"

**Success state:** Created something they're proud of (or at least honest about), received something that surprised them, walked out of the meetup knowing 2–3 people they didn't know before. Wants to do it again.

## Secondary Persona: The Returning Participant

**Who:** Someone who has completed 1–3 seasons. Knows the format. May know several people in the broader pool from previous meetups. Gets reshuffled into a new cohort each season.

**Goal:** Deepen self-expression over time. Connect with new people while maintaining ties formed in previous seasons. Experience the ritual's evolution — same structure, different humans, different creation each time.

**Constraints:** Familiarity can breed complacency ("I'll just do what I did last time"). The optional theme may feel less necessary. The reshuffling means no guaranteed continuity with specific people.

**Success state:** Each season's creation feels different from the last. The meetup still produces surprise. The community feels like it's growing in quality, not just size.

## Tertiary Persona: The Inviter

**Who:** An existing participant who brings someone new to the organizer for in-person registration. They're implicitly recommending the new person, though the organizer makes the final call.

**Goal:** Share something they value with someone they think would appreciate it. Grow the community with people who fit.

**Constraints:** Social risk — if their invitee flakes, it reflects on them. No formal gatekeeping criteria beyond the organizer's judgment.

**Success state:** Their invitee has a good experience. The inviter sees the community grow with someone who contributes to it.

## Quaternary Persona: The Organizer

**Who:** Solo operator. Builds and runs the community, develops the app, manages the logistics, attends every meetup, and sometimes participates in the loop as a creator.

**Goal:** Build a community of authentic relationships in Kyiv through a repeatable ritual. Scale through parallel cohorts without losing intimacy. Eventually grow a small team.

**Constraints:** Solo — every operational task is on one person. The app, the venue, the SMS costs, the social graph, the failure protocols, the meetup facilitation. Time is the binding constraint, not money.

**Fears:** "What if nobody sends?" "What if the meetup falls flat?" "What if operational overhead kills my capacity to participate?" "What if I'm unavailable and there's no one to run this?"

**Success state:** The ritual runs smoothly enough that the community grows by word of mouth, participants return season after season, and the organizer's role shrinks over time as the format becomes self-evident.

## Jobs to Be Done

1. **Newcomer:** "I want to express myself authentically to a stranger, in a context where that's normal and safe, so I can break out of my social bubble."
2. **Returning participant:** "I want a recurring structure that pushes me to create and connect, without it becoming routine or performative."
3. **Inviter:** "I want to share this experience with someone I think would value it, knowing the structure will do the work of making it comfortable."
4. **Organizer:** "I want to build a community of authentic relationships in Kyiv, using a repeatable ritual that scales through parallel cohorts without losing intimacy."

## Anti-Personas (NOT building for)

- **The content creator:** Someone who wants an audience, likes, or public validation. The single-recipient model is the opposite of what they want.
- **The passive consumer:** Someone who wants to receive without creating. The loop requires both directions.
- **The optimizer:** Someone who wants to "win" at self-expression or compare quality. The structure eliminates comparison by design, but this person will fight the structure.
- **The no-commitment joiner:** Someone who signs up for things and doesn't follow through. The ready-confirm gate and soft exclusion are specifically designed to filter this person out over time.
